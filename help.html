Data Pill Guide

Quick answers for setting up your data plan, reading your pill, adding widgets, and keeping your records backed up.

Basics Streak Pill Widget Notifications Backup Data Languages

Basics

What is a Data Plan?

A data plan is a service provided by mobile carriers, offering users internet access on their mobile devices for a set fee.

How to Set the Data Plan?

You need to set the period and the data amount your plan has. All of this can be edited from the Data Plan card.

How to Disable Data Plan?

You can disable the data plan using the switch if you just want to monitor data on a daily basis.

Difference Between Manual & Auto Period?

  • Auto: Automatically sets the next billing cycle or period.
  • Manual: You set the period yourself.

Note: Plan Period dates will be highlighted in red if today's date is not in range with the period selected.

Difference Between Daily & Plan Usage?

  • Daily usage refers to the amount of data consumed within a single day.
  • Plan usage covers the total data usage within your mobile carrier billing cycle.

What is Data Amount?

It is the maximum amount of internet data you can use within your billing cycle without incurring extra charges or experiencing reduced speeds.

What is Untracked Amount?

It is the amount of data usage from your mobile internet plan that occurred before you installed Data Pill. This added amount will be included in your total usage for the current period.

What is Daily Limit?

The daily threshold set by the app to notify you if you've exceeded the daily limit through a notification.

What is Plan Limit?

Similar to the Daily Limit, but applies to the entire plan period.

Streak

What is a Streak?

A Streak tracks how many consecutive days you have stayed within your daily usage limit. Each day you avoid exceeding your daily limit, your streak continues.

Streaks require a valid Daily Limit. If Data Plan is enabled, today also needs to be inside your current plan period.

How do Streak Rewards work?

When you reach certain milestones, such as 7, 14, 30, or 60 days, you can claim a reward. Each reward unlocks Super Trial for 7 days, so you can experience Data Pill's premium features.

If you claim another reward while Super Trial is active, the app extends your trial.

Pill

What is a Pill?

A Pill is a visual indicator that represents your daily or plan usage. It allows you to quickly see your data consumption.

How to Show Daily Usage for the Week?

Tap the Pill, then swipe left to view daily usage for the past 7 days.

How to Show All Daily Usage for the Current Period?

Tap the Pill, then scroll down to see the full breakdown of your usage for the current billing period.

Widget

How to Add Widget to Homescreen?

To add a widget on the homescreen, long-press the home screen then tap the + button at the top-right corner of the screen. Search "Data Pill" widget and choose the size you prefer.

How to Add Widget to Lockscreen?

To add a widget on the lockscreen, long-press the lockscreen then tap "Customize". Tap the bottom widget area and add the "Data Pill" widget.

How to Add to StandBy?

To add a widget on StandBy screen, long-press the screen while on StandBy display. Unlock your phone then tap the + button at the top-left corner of the screen. Search "Data Pill" widget and tap "Add Widget" then tap "Done".

Why I can't see the Plan Usage?

You need to edit the widget from the homescreen by long-pressing it. Set Usage Type to Daily to see today's usage, or Plan to see your overall usage for the current period or cycle.

For small widgets, you can also change Small Widget to Data Usage or Days Left. You can add another widget with a different configuration, such as days left in the period.

Why Widget is not Updating?

Widgets typically update every 5 minutes. However, updates might be delayed if you're not actively using the app or if you've reached the daily limit of 40-60 refreshes.

Why My Widget is Blank / Black?

On iOS 17, the widget may turn black after updating the app. This is an iOS issue. To fix this, restart your device or remove and re-add the widget on the homescreen.

Notifications

Why Notifications are Not Working?

Ensure notifications are enabled in both places:

  • Device Settings:Settings > Data Pill > Notifications > Allow Notifications
  • App Settings:Settings > Notifications > Daily Usage & Plan Usage

Backup

How to Backup & Restore my Records?

To backup your data, enable "Data Pill" backup in Settings.

Settings > [Your Name] > [This Device] > iCloud Backup > [This Device Backup] > Enable "Data Pill"

With Super Pill, you can also back up your data manually by exporting it to a CSV file from Records.

Settings > Show All Records > Export to CSV

When restoring from a CSV file, the first two columns are required. Extra columns from Data Pill exports are accepted and ignored during import.

  • Required columns: Date and Used Amount in MB.
  • Date format should be d MMM yyyy. Month names can be in German, Turkish, Chinese, Filipino, or English.
  • Used Amount in MB must be a valid number.
  • Keep the file structure exactly the same for smooth importing.

Data

Why is the Usage on App Not Accurate?

There might be a +/- 1-5 MB difference due to API limitations. Check your phone's settings for exact data usage.

How does the App Calculate the Speed?

When "Tracking Data Amount Throughout the Day" is enabled in Settings, the app attempts to record the amount of data used every 5 minutes, or based on the selected time interval. However, this tracking is not always precise, as widget updates depend on the system's refresh schedule. If some intervals are missed, the app averages the available data.

Settings > Data Updates > Track Data Amount Throughout the Day

For example, if you've used 0.1 MB over the last 3 intervals, the app will calculate the average as 0.1 MB / 3 = 0.033 MB. All non-cumulative data points collected throughout the day are averaged and displayed as the speed, such as x MB / 5 min.

Why it is not tracking my data usage?

To track your data usage while the app is not open, you need to add the widget to your home screen or lock screen.

For throughout-the-day tracking and speed statistics, you also need Super Pill and the Data Updates tracking setting enabled.

The widget updates your data usage only when it is refreshed by the system. If the widget isn't added or hasn't been refreshed, the data won't update.
